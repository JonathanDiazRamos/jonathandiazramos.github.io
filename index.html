Master's Thesis

A Machine Learning Approach to Recognize Environmental Features Associated with Social Factors

This thesis enhances the Climate and Economic Justice Screening Tool (CEJST) by extracting key environmental features—garbage bags, greenery, and three types of road damage (longitudinal, transverse, and alligator cracks)—from Google Street View (GSV) images using deep learning techniques. These features were analyzed across three cities to differentiate disadvantaged from non-disadvantaged census tracts, revealing the approach’s efficiency, detail, and cost-effectiveness. Can computer vision help reveal deeper insights into the socioeconomic fabric of our communities?
